Stage 01 · 1,200m

The Trailhead

Where the forest swallows the path

The sign at the trailhead is weathered beyond easy reading. Someone has carved their initials into the wooden post below it. The pines grow so close together that even midday light arrives as thin, amber needles through the canopy. The air smells of resin and cold earth. You tighten your straps and begin.

Stage 02 · 1,800m

The Foothills Open

The range reveals itself for the first time

Above the treeline's first shelf, the path flattens and you turn to look back. The valley is already small. Ahead, the main range emerges from its own shadow — ridge after ridge folded into distance, each a paler shade of itself. You count four visible layers. The summit is somewhere behind the last one.

Stage 03 · 2,600m

The Rock Begins

No more soil. Only stone.

The switchbacks here are brutal and honest — no shortcuts, no shorthand. Each hairpin gains thirty metres of altitude and costs ten minutes of breath. Vegetation is gone except for sparse, tough grass in the crack lines. Above the next crest: grey sky and darker grey rock, and the sound of your own footsteps becoming very loud.

Stage 04 · 3,200m

Into the White

The clouds have a floor and you have crossed it

Visibility contracts to fifty metres, then thirty. The light becomes sourceless — bright everywhere, from nowhere. You cannot tell where rock ends and cloud begins. Then, for thirty seconds, the mist parts and the sun falls through in a single column, illuminating a ridge two hundred metres away as if it were a stage set. Then the curtain closes again.

Stage 05 · 3,800m

The Snowline

Permanent ice. The cold is structural now.

The first patches appear in north-facing gullies, thin and hard. By this altitude they are continuous. Your footprints are the only ones today. The rock beneath is glassy with old ice and the silence has a different quality — less the absence of sound than the presence of something else entirely. The peaks above are very clear, very close, and very far.

Summit · 4,200m

The Summit

Everything below is the world. You are above it.

The cairn is larger than expected, built by many hands over many years. You add your stone. The horizon is so wide and so far below that it curves — or you imagine it does. The sun, barely risen, turns the snowfield below into hammered gold. There is no wind at this exact moment. You allow yourself to stand still.
